Leadership In Action
Delegation, Empowerment & Self-Assessment

What is Giving Jobs to Others? (Delegation)

Imagine you have a big box of toys to tidy up. That's a big job for one person, right?

Delegation is like asking your friend, "Can you help put all the red blocks in this basket?" You're giving them a small part of your big job to do. You're sharing the work so it gets done faster and everyone can help!

It's about trusting someone else to do a part of the task.

Making Others Feel Strong! (Empowerment)

Empowerment is like when you're building a super cool castle with blocks, and your teacher says, "You decide how tall the towers should be! I know you can make it amazing!"

It's about giving someone the power and confidence to make their own choices and do things their way. When people feel empowered, they feel smart, trusted, and happy to do their best work!

Good Sharing vs. Watching Too Closely

Good sharing (Delegation) is like being a team captain in a game. You pass the ball to your teammates because you trust them to try and score. Everyone gets to play, learn, and help the team win! This helps your teammates grow and feel important.

Uh Oh! Watching Too Closely (Micromanagement)

But imagine a captain who grabs the ball from everyone and shouts, "No, do it exactly like this! Point your toe that way!" That's micromanaging. It's like someone watching over your shoulder for every tiny thing you do. It makes teammates feel like they can't do anything right, it's not fun, and they don't get to learn or be creative.

Nice Ways to Ask for Help (Useful Phrases)

When you want to give someone a job from your big task, you can say nice things like:

  • "Would you like to be in charge of building the tower?" (Taking ownership)
  • "Let's talk about how you can be the main helper for drawing the background." (Taking responsibility)
  • "Do you think you can sort these crayons all by yourself?" (Handling a task)
  • "I know you'll choose the best colors for this picture!" (Making the final decision)
  • "Could you ask Tim and Sara to help with cutting out shapes so we finish on time?" (Delegating some tasks)
  • "Let's help everyone feel good about choosing how to decorate our poster!" (Empowering the team)

Friendly Tip!

Remember to say it with a smile and a friendly voice! Being kind with your words and how you look helps people feel trusted and happy to help. (Inspired by PDF page 6)

Tips for Being a Great Job-Sharer!

Here are some ideas for when you share jobs (from PDF page 7's checklist):

  • Choose wisely: Is this a good job to share? Is it something another person can do?
  • Pick the right helper: Does your friend know how to do it? Do they want to try?
  • Explain clearly: Tell them what you need, when you need it by, and what the finished job should look like. Like giving clear instructions for a game!
  • Trust them: Let them try their way. You can help if they ask, but trust them to make choices.
  • Check in kindly: See how they're doing (but don't watch every second!). When they're done, say "Great job!" or help them learn if it wasn't quite right.

More Cool Words for Leaders!

Here are some other cool words and ideas from the grown-up guide (PDF page 8):

  • Balanced Workload: This means not giving one person too many toys to tidy up, while another has only one. Share the jobs fairly!
  • "We can't guess thoughts!": It's important to talk and tell people what you need. Don't expect them to read your mind!
  • "Bang-up job!": This is a fun way to say "Great work!" or "Awesome job!"
  • Taking Ownership: This means someone says, "Yes, this is MY part of the job, and I'll make sure it's done well!"

Are You a Good Team Captain?

Being a good leader is like being a good friend or a great team captain. How can you tell if you're doing a good job? You can think about yourself! (This is from PDF page 9)

Seeing Yourself (Self-Awareness)

This is like looking in a magic mirror that shows you what you're really like. Do you know what makes you happy or sad? Do you know what you're good at, or what's tricky for you? Knowing yourself is super important!

Understanding Feelings (Emotional Intelligence)

This is like having special feelers that help you understand your own feelings and other people's feelings too. If your friend is sad, can you tell? Do you know how to make them feel a bit better? That's using your heart-smarts!

Different Kinds of Captains (Leadership Styles)

Leaders can be like different kinds of game organizers. There isn't just one way to be a leader! (From PDF page 9)

  • Bossy Bear (Autocratic): This leader is like a bear who says, "We play MY game, by MY rules. Just do what I say!" They decide everything. Sometimes this is quick, but not always the most fun for others.
  • Friendly Fox (Democratic): This leader is like a clever fox who asks, "What game do YOU all want to play? Let's talk about it and pick together!" They like to hear everyone's ideas. This makes everyone feel included.
  • Inspiring Eagle (Transformational): This leader is like an eagle soaring high! They get everyone excited about a big, amazing goal and help each person feel like they can do incredible things and be their best. They love new ideas and big changes!

Let's Think About It!

Time to use your brain and think about these leader things (from PDF page 7):

  • How do you feel when someone lets YOU choose how to do something (like clean your room or draw a picture)?
  • Is it good to let your friends or teammates make choices? Why or why not?
  • Have you ever had someone watch you super closely while you were trying to do something? How did that make you feel?
  • How can a leader (like a teacher or team captain) show they trust their team when they give out jobs?
  • Think about a time you asked someone to help you. Did you let them choose enough on their own?
  • What are 3 new ways you can try to be better at sharing jobs and helping others feel strong?
